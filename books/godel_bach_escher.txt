--- Key Quotes ---

- Consistency is not a property of a formal system, per se, but depends on the
  interpretation which is proposed for it. (p. 94)
- Instead of just considering programs composed of procedures which can
  recursively call themselves, why not get really sophisticated, and invent
  programs which can modify themselves --- programs which can act on programs,
  extending them, improving them, generalizing them, fixing them, and so on?
  This kind of "tangled recursion" probably lies at the heart of
  intelligence. (p. 152)
- [Four words needed for propositional logic: "and", "if...then...", "or",
  "not"]
